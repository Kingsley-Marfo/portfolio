Accessibility is often treated as a compliance checkbox. Built in from the start,
it's actually a usability upgrade for every user.

## Semantics first

Use the right element for the job — a `<button>` is focusable, keyboard-operable
and announced correctly for free. Reinventing it with a `<div>` throws all of
that away.

## Keyboard is the baseline

If it works with a keyboard alone, it works for a huge range of assistive tech.
During the **OpFix** accessibility pass, fixing keyboard traps and adding visible
focus improved the experience for mouse users too.

## Label everything

Every input, icon button and control needs an accessible name. Screen-reader
users navigate by those names.

## The lesson

WCAG isn't a ceiling to scrape under — it's a floor that makes your product
better for everyone standing on it.
